Redistributing Surplus Food to Combat Hunger

Surplus food from restaurants, grocery stores, and farms often ends up in landfills. However, this food can be redirected to those in need.

Redistribution efforts address both environmental concerns and social challenges, creating a win-win solution.

Examples of Food Rescue Programs and Food Banks

  • City Harvest: Rescues surplus food in New York City and delivers it to food banks.
  • The Felix Project: Redistributes surplus food to charities in London.
  • Second Harvest: Provides meals to those in need across Canada.
