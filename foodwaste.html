Understanding and Tackling Food Waste

What is Food Waste?

Food waste refers to food that is discarded or lost uneaten. This can occur at various stages of the food supply chain including production, processing, retailing, and consumption. It's a global issue that impacts food security, the environment, and the economy.

Causes of Food Waste

  • Overproduction in agriculture and food services
  • Improper storage and transportation
  • Confusion over expiration labels (e.g., "best before" vs "use by")
  • Consumer behavior such as overbuying or picky eating
  • Inefficiencies in food processing and packaging

Reliable Solutions

  • Educating consumers on proper food storage and label interpretation
  • Donating surplus food to food banks and shelters
  • Improving supply chain efficiency and inventory management
  • Composting organic waste instead of sending it to landfills
  • Supporting local and seasonal food purchasing to reduce spoilage
